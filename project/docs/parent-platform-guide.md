# Parent Platform Guide

This guide explains how a parent uses the Penthouse platform for daycare, after-school, and program transportation.

## 1. What this platform does

The platform helps parents:

- subscribe for transportation service
- enroll a child even before transportation is needed right now
- keep child pickup and dropoff information current
- receive ride updates
- follow the driver with a tracking link
- understand incentives, credits, and billing

Think of it like this:

- the daycare or program signs up on the platform as an admin account
- the daycare or program manages the transportation relationship
- the dispatch team runs the trips
- the parent keeps the child information and contact details accurate

## 2. Why parents should sign up early

Parents should sign up even if they do not need service this week.

Why:

- the child can be ready for future rides without last-minute paperwork
- dispatch already has the pickup, dropoff, and guardian information
- the family can use the platform later for appointments, programs, sports, and special events
- some children cannot use regular bus transportation, so it helps to be prepared before the ride is urgent

Good examples of future rides:

- doctor or therapy appointments
- after-school programs
- football games and other sports
- summer programs
- emergency schedule changes
- one-off rides when school bus service is not an option

## 3. How a parent gets started

1. Join the program through the daycare or provider.
2. Make sure the daycare or program is already signed up on the platform as an admin account.
3. Enroll the child even if rides are only needed in the future.
4. Confirm the child name, pickup address, dropoff address, and guardian contact number.
5. Choose the subscription plan if the provider offers one.
6. Ask the provider or dispatch team how trip updates will be shared.
7. Save the rider tracking link when a trip is live.

## 4. What parents should have ready

Before the first trip, or before future rides are requested, the parent should be ready to provide:

- child full name
- parent or guardian full name
- best phone number for pickup problems
- backup contact if the main guardian is unavailable
- full pickup address
- full dropoff address
- mobility or behavior notes the driver must know
- release instructions if the child cannot be dropped off without an adult

## 5. How subscriptions work

A parent subscription can cover both current and future transportation access.

Common plan setup:

- `Monthly`: steady weekly daycare or school rides
- `Annual`: lower monthly cost for long-term riders
- `Trial`: short first period before full activation

Parents should confirm:

- monthly price
- whether autopay is enabled
- when billing starts
- whether there is a signup credit or referral reward
- whether the child stays dispatch-ready even before active rides begin
- who to contact before canceling or pausing service

## 6. Parent incentive program

If the provider enables the parent incentive program, parents may receive:

- a signup credit for joining the platform
- a referral reward when another family joins
- a ride credit or account credit after staying active for the required number of months

The point of the incentive is simple:

- get families enrolled early
- keep their information ready
- make future dispatch easier when a ride is needed later

Parents should ask these questions:

- What reward do I get for subscribing early?
- How long do I need to stay active to keep the reward?
- Is there a referral code?
- Does the reward lower my bill or apply to future rides?

## 7. Future-use enrollment

Even if a child does not need rides today, the parent should still keep the enrollment current.

That means:

- keep contact information updated
- update addresses before a ride is needed
- keep release rules current
- tell the provider if the child may need transportation later for events, appointments, or programs

This prevents the usual mess where a family suddenly needs a ride and dispatch has nothing ready.

## 8. Trip day expectations

On ride days, parents should:

- have the child ready before pickup time
- keep the phone nearby in case dispatch calls
- review any same-day provider messages
- check the rider tracking link once the ride is active

If something changes:

- call the provider or dispatch team as early as possible
- do not wait until the driver arrives

## 9. Using the rider tracking link

The rider tracking link lets the parent:

- see when the driver is on the way
- know when the vehicle arrives
- follow the ride in progress
- confirm dropoff status

Best practice:

- save the link on the phone
- share it only with people approved to follow the trip

## 10. Safety and release rules

Parents must clearly tell the provider:

- who can receive the child at dropoff
- whether the child must be released to an adult
- whether there are gate codes, apartment access rules, or school handoff instructions
- whether the child needs a booster, wheelchair support, or an aide

If the provider has a strict guardian-release policy, the driver should not be expected to guess. Parents need to give exact instructions before service begins.

## 11. When to contact support

Parents should contact the provider or dispatch team if:

- pickup address changed
- dropoff address changed
- the child will be absent
- the child now needs a future ride that was not planned before
- the tracking link is missing
- the ride is late
- the wrong contact information is on file
- billing looks wrong

## 12. Quick parent checklist

- Sign up even if rides are only needed later.
- Make sure child and guardian details are correct.
- Confirm plan price and billing status.
- Ask whether a signup incentive or referral reward is active.
- Ask whether enrollment keeps the child ready for future rides like appointments, programs, or sports.
- Save the rider tracking link when a trip is active.
- Report schedule changes early, not last minute.
