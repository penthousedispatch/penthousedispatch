# Penthouse Dispatch Privacy Policy Outline

Use this as the structure for your production privacy policy page. This is not legal advice, but it gives you the exact sections your app should include before store submission.

## 1. Introduction

Explain:
- Penthouse Dispatch provides dispatch, company operations, driver tools, rider tracking, and related transportation workflow services.
- This privacy policy explains what information is collected, how it is used, and what choices users have.

Suggested opening:

Penthouse Dispatch respects the privacy of transportation providers, dispatch teams, drivers, riders, and other users of our platform. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information when you use our website, mobile applications, and related services.

## 2. Who This Policy Applies To

List:
- administrators
- company users
- drivers
- riders
- visitors to the website

## 3. Information We Collect

Break into categories.

### Account Information

- name
- email address
- phone number
- role or account type
- company affiliation

### Operational and Dispatch Information

- trip details
- dispatch assignments
- rider pickup and dropoff information
- driver trip status updates
- scheduling and routing settings

### Location Information

- precise driver location while using the app
- rider tracking location context where applicable
- map interaction and route-related data

### Device and Usage Information

- browser/device type
- IP address
- basic diagnostics
- app activity logs

### Uploaded Content

- driver profile photos
- company documents
- onboarding attachments
- other files provided by users

### Payment and Banking Information

Explain carefully:
- Penthouse Dispatch may display or store limited payout-related data such as bank last 4 or payout status.
- Sensitive financial account connection and verification should be handled through secure third-party providers where applicable.
- Do not claim you store less than you actually do.

### Compliance and Identity Information

Explain carefully:
- If driver onboarding or company onboarding involves compliance-related information, only list what is actually collected.
- If full SSNs are not stored in the app, say that clearly.

Suggested line:

Penthouse Dispatch is designed to avoid storing full Social Security numbers in the core application when secure third-party identity or payroll verification providers are used. Where applicable, the platform may store limited verification-related data such as status or last 4 digits only.

## 4. How We Use Information

Include:
- provide and operate the platform
- authenticate users
- manage dispatch and trip workflows
- support rider tracking and driver operations
- process onboarding and compliance workflows
- support payouts and billing operations
- improve app performance and user experience
- detect misuse, fraud, or security issues
- communicate service updates and support messages

## 5. How We Share Information

Include only what is true.

Possible sections:
- with the user’s company or transportation provider
- with service providers that support hosting, authentication, storage, messaging, mapping, or payments
- with integration partners chosen by the customer, such as transportation workflow systems
- when required by law or legal process
- in connection with a business transfer

Important:
- if you are not selling data, say so clearly

Suggested line:

Penthouse Dispatch does not sell personal information to data brokers or third-party advertisers.

## 6. Integrations and Service Providers

List categories like:
- cloud hosting
- authentication
- mapping/location services
- payment or payout services
- analytics or diagnostics
- transportation-system integrations

Do not over-promise invisibility; just describe categories unless you want to name providers.

## 7. Location Data

This needs to be explicit because store reviewers care about it.

Suggested section:

Driver and rider location-related information may be used to power dispatch operations, routing, driver visibility, trip coordination, and rider tracking. Location data is used for platform functionality, safety, and service delivery, not for third-party advertising.

## 8. Data Retention

Suggested points:
- retain account and operational data as needed to provide services
- retain records for compliance, support, audit, and dispute resolution
- remove or anonymize data when no longer required, subject to legal or contractual obligations

## 9. Security

Suggested section:

Penthouse Dispatch uses administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 10. User Rights and Choices

Include:
- account updates
- password changes
- access/deletion requests where applicable
- communications preferences
- location permission controls at the device level

## 11. Children’s Privacy

Standard section:
- not directed to children under 13
- no knowing collection from children under 13 without lawful basis

## 12. International Users

If you want, include a short section that the service is operated in the United States and data may be processed there.

## 13. Changes to This Policy

Say:
- policy may be updated from time to time
- revised date will be updated

## 14. Contact Information

Include:
- support email
- company/business name
- website/domain

Suggested footer contact:
- support@penthousedps.com
- https://www.penthousedps.com
